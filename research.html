Research

Shellfish, particularly oysters, play an important role in top-down control of eutrophication - excessive nutrient enrichment of water bodies can lead to a number harmful problems e.g. algal blooms.

They are filter feeders: they actively remove nutrients from the water column in the form of plankton and other organic material.

They provide a number of ecosystem services: removing nutrients for the water column allows sunlight to penetrate the surface to greater depths, this provides energy for seagrasses to photosynthesize, which in turn provide habitat a great number of other marines species in the larval stage.

My colleagues and I work with a number of local oyster farmers to obtain access to our sampling sites. Samples are then analyzed to obtain temporal and spatial data on nutrient concentrations.
